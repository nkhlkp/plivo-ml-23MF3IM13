LLM Speedrun Submission Summary

1. Architecture & Parameter Optimization

To maximize performance strictly under the 2,000,000 parameter and 2,000 step limits, the baseline GPT-2 mechanics were heavily modified to align with modern LLM standards. The architecture was upgraded to utilize RMSNorm (replacing LayerNorm to skip mean-centering and speed up CPU matrix math) and SwiGLU blocks (replacing standard MLPs for significantly higher expressivity per parameter).

To solve the Devanagari sequence bottleneck, where Hindi characters were consuming 3 bytes each, a custom Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokenizer was implemented. The tokenizer was trained on a 256KB subset of the corpus to prevent CPU hanging while effectively compressing the context window.

2. Runlog Summary & Metric Defense

The development process consisted of six tracked iterations. Early iterations focused on scaling dimensions, which initially failed due to exploding residual variance and noisy gradients from small batch sizes. The breakthrough occurred by implementing gradient accumulation (for a stable effective batch size) and a LambdaLR Cosine Scheduler with AdamW.

Why this is the best metric: The final architecture dropped the BPB (Bits Per Byte) score from the 2.37 baseline down to 2.2484. This was achieved not by brute force, but by mathematically increasing the information density per sequence step (via BPE) and optimizing parameter allocation (via SwiGLU/Weight Tying) for maximum learning capacity within the constrained environment.
